About Cigar Nexus

An interactive exploration of the relationships that shape the premium cigar world.

What is Cigar Nexus?

Cigar Nexus is a visual tool designed to map the complex web of relationships in the premium cigar industry. Using an interactive force-directed graph, it reveals connections between brands, factories, families, and corporate owners — with a particular focus on the major producers in Nicaragua and the Dominican Republic.

Why It Exists

The cigar world is surprisingly small and deeply interconnected. Many "boutique" brands are produced in the same factories, owned by the same families, or use tobacco from the same growers. These relationships are rarely obvious from packaging or marketing.

Cigar Nexus was built to make these hidden connections visible — whether you're a collector trying to understand the landscape, an enthusiast curious about provenance, or someone who simply enjoys seeing how the pieces fit together.

What You'll Find

  • Ownership structures and corporate relationships
  • Contract manufacturing arrangements between brands and factories
  • Family dynasties and multi-generational operations
  • Key players in the Nicaraguan and Dominican ecosystems
  • Historical context behind many of today's most respected names

The Data

The information shown here is compiled from public sources, industry reporting, and extensive research. While we strive for accuracy, the cigar industry is private and relationships can shift. This is a living project — we regularly update nodes and connections as new information becomes available.

Note: This is not an exhaustive database, nor is it affiliated with any manufacturer or distributor.

How to Use the Map

Click any node to see detailed information, including its relationships and notable cigars. Use the filters and search bar to narrow the view. Zoom and pan to explore the full network. The graph is designed to be explored — drag nodes around to rearrange the view.

This project is a passion project built for the cigar community. If you spot an error, have a suggestion, or would like to contribute research, feel free to reach out.
